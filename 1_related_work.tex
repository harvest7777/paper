\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

Graph visualization algorithms have been extensively studied, with foundational 
work by \cite{gansner1993technique} establishing the DOT algorithm for 
hierarchical graph layout. Parallel optimization of graph algorithms has been 
explored through GPU acceleration~\cite{merrill2012scalable} and distributed computing approaches~\cite{shun2013ligra}. The application of artificial intelligence to 
performance optimization represents a rapidly evolving field, with machine 
learning approaches for automatic parallelization \cite{cummins2017end} and 
AI-driven compiler optimization \cite{ashouri2018survey} demonstrating 
significant potential for intelligent optimization strategies. OpenMP 
performance characteristics on ARM architectures have received increased 
attention with Apple's M1 processor, where research by 
\cite{jin2011high} revealed architecture-specific optimization 
opportunities that differ from traditional x86 approaches, particularly 
regarding unified memory architecture considerations. The DOT layout algorithm 
\cite{gansner1993technique} proceeds in four phases: (1) ranking nodes, 
(2) minimizing edge crossings, (3) coordinate assignment, and (4) final layout 
refinement, with each phase consisting of computational kernels such as rank 
assignment, transpose operations, and crossing minimization that form the basis 
of our optimization study.
